Top Three Cat Breeds

If you were wondering about getting a cat (or just curious learning about some cat breeds), you've come to the right page! You will learn about three different cat breeds that might just win over your heart.

Let's get on with the list!

Somali


The Somali Cat is often referred to as a "fox cat" due to its large ears and bushy tail. Physical exercise is important to it's health since it is a more active breed, so make sure you can fulfill this need before getting one. It's also considered a very intelligent breed with high wit.

On that note. when considering getting a Somali, make sure to keep this list of it's attributes in mind:
  1. Very demure.
  2. Very cute.
  3. Very fluffy.

Onto the next breed!

Ragdoll


A ragdoll is a pretty popular option for people who look into adopting a cat. It's considered a very affectionate and gentle breed with a docile temperament. They get their name from the way the go limp when they get picked up, basically ragdolling. These adorable, fluffy companions make amazing roomates, it's no wonder they're popular.

Oh, but please consider these:
  • fur, everywhere. *goodluck with grooming*
  • A predispotion to various hereditary diseases. :(
(Alright, here's a positive one):
  • You'll have a best bud that will make your days brighter!

Thank you, next!

Abyssinian


Believed to be a descendent of ancient Egyptian's cats, it has big ears and tawny, short-haired fur. They're very loyal companians that attach themselves to their owners, having a playful and energetic disposition. They're witty and intelligent, and they love having your attention. It's important to note, however, that they can become depressed if they feel like they aren't getting enough of your attention, so it's important to consider how much time you would have for them before adopting.
